Model comparison

Pre-match models predict before a ball is bowled (near-random — see the caveat below); dynamic models update ball-by-ball as the match unfolds and are dramatically more accurate. Lower Brier score is better; higher AUC is better.

Pre-match win prediction is close to a coin flip (internal cross-validated AUC ≈ 0.51). The dynamic (in-game) model is where this pipeline is genuinely strong — AUC 0.878 once the match is underway.

Score regression

Predicting the final innings total. Pre-match score prediction genuinely doesn't work (negative R² — worse than just guessing the average score); once the innings is underway, it becomes far more reliable.

Negative R² for every pre-match model is expected, not a bug. Score prediction before a ball is bowled is genuinely close to guessing — documented in this project's own known-limitations notes.
